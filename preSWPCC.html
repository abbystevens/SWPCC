Rebecca Mikami

I believe that we are all students and life-long learners. It is this curiosity driven, joy of doing approach that allows me to be a fun and creative teacher. For me teaching preschoolers is about guiding discovery and being immersed in the act and joy of doing – be it feeling the stickiness of paint, watching a new chick hatch out of its egg, digging in the fresh soil of the garden, building a bird nest with twigs and play dough, creating the most amazing pretend castle with blocks, stirring applesauce with friends, or snuggling up for a favorite story. By guiding children though their own discoveries and supporting their curiosity, we allow them to learn through play and likely exceed any bounds we would have set for them.

I’m an innately curious person, especially about development, communication, and the human experience, so I’m so happy that I have a job that allows me to funnel my own curiosity and playful spirit. I genuinely love to interact with children and adults and love to celebrate each person’s achievements. And each day at the preschool there is so much to celebrate!

I joined the SWPCC family as a parent in 2005 and joined the co-op teaching team as a PS teacher in 2012. I received my BA in Communication from the University of Puget Sound and my Masters in Science in Speech-Language Pathology from the University of Oregon. I am a nationally certified Speech-Language Pathologist, although I currently have an inactive state license. I have experience working with children in a variety of settings from preschools, to hospitals, to clinics, to high schools. In addition to teaching, I also own and run a family portrait photography studio in SW Portland. My two children, who both attended SWPCC, are now in elementary school. When I have free time, I love to garden, watercolor paint, do photography, go cycling with my husband Jamie, and explore the beautiful natural areas of the Pacific Northwest.

Marcia Liebe

Welcome and in the words of one of this year’s children, “What’s the theme today?” The preschool at the Southwest Parent Child Collective spends chunks of time learning about various topics. Themes give purpose to learning. Our four-hour day gives us plenty of time for child directed play and we frequently have sick kitties and doggies at our veterinarian clinic!

Recently one of the parents helping out commented I must have had a lot of fun playing while growing up. Upon reflection that is true and I am thankful to have found a job that values play. This is my seventh year teaching in the preschool, but my thirteenth year teaching at SWPCC. My three (now adult!) children were all attended SWPCC while I was a teacher in the Toddler Room.

I have a BA in elementary/special education and have a broad range of teaching experience. My work with children with behavioral and learning challenges has helped my understanding of Occupational Therapy/Sensory needs. I have taught math, social studies, and science to small groups of high school students. Currently, I also have a small business teaching students with math deficits. These experiences help me understand the types of challenges preschool children will face in later years. The rewards for building the foundation for that future learning are joy and satisfaction for me and each preschooler who plays along.

We offer competitive tuition rates. The following rates and fees are for the 2013-2014 school year and are voted on by the co-op families each Spring for the following year. There may be financial aid if necessary.

Registration fee:

$85 non-refundable registration fee (annual)

Tuition:

$103 per month X number of days your child is at co-op without their parent. Example: if your child attends SWPCC on a three day/week schedule, you pay for two of those days because you are not billed for your shift day. 2 x $103 = $206, so your monthly tuition would be $206.

Annual Insurance coverage:

$85 (subject to change)
